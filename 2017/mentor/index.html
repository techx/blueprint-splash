Interested in mentoring or teaching for Blueprint, a weekend-long learnathon and hackathon for local high school students? Help teach curious students and inspire them to pursue computer science!

ABOUT

The goal of Blueprint is to teach high school students with basic computer science backgrounds how to develop and code a functional project (from start to finish). We need your help to make this happen! We are looking for undergrads, grads, and professionals to fill the following roles:

teachers

Learnathon Only (March 4)

  • Lead a hands-on workshop on iOS, Android, or web development (depending on student interest)
  • Workshop should be no longer than 90 minutes
  • We'll work with you on developing a lesson plan!
  • Or, you can teach a short "tech talk" on a specialized topic in computer science! (Example topics could be computer vision, machine learning, or anything else you're passionate about.)

mentors

Learnathon/Hackathon (March 5)

  • On the learnathon day, mentors will act as TAs, moving from group to group and helping them with any questions or roadblocks they have
  • On the hackathon day, mentors will help students with their own hacks
  • Mentorship will be facilitated by HELPq

FAQ

Who can mentor/teach?

We welcome any undergraduate or graduate students interested! We can't provide travel reimbursement, but non-MIT college students are still invited to sign up.

Can I teach and mentor?

Yes! You can do any combination you'd like.

Can I help out on Saturday only? Sunday only? Both days?

Yes!

What kind of background will the high school students have?

Students will have basic, foundational computer science backgrounds — think AP Computer Science or its equivalent.

What kind of background should teachers have?

Workshops on the learnathon day will be focused on iOS, Android, or web development, so workshop teachers and mentors should have experience in one (or more) of those topics! Tech talks can be on any computer science topic.

What kind of background should mentors have?

Mentors on the learnathon day will act as TAs for the classes, so experience with iOS, Android, or web development is preferred. For the hackathon day, mentors with a wider range of experience are welcome.

If you have other questions, please email us at blueprint@hackmit.org! We're happy to help.
